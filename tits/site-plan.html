Overview

Purpose

We render timely and high quality translation services from the English source to the targeted IsiXhosa language. IsiXhosa is a Nguni language and is one of the official languages from the eleven languages spoken in South Africa. It is spoken approximately by 8.2 million people and by other 11 million as a second language in South Africa, mostly in Eastern Cape, Western Cape, Gauteng and Northern Cape. Our intent is to provide prompt and reliable translations or interpretations of written or spoken thoughts between languages. We translate all kinds of documents, manuals, scriptural and non-scriptural texts, audio visuals etc. Our translations are presently focused on IsiXhosa as a target language. As the company expands in the future, our vision is to have on board professional translators of the other South African official languages. We also intend on expanding the business in 2023 by offering online IsiXhosa lessons to the world.

Audience

Corporate and small businesses, educational institutions, government, courts, churches, and individuals local and world-wide whose intention is to sell or render services to the native Xhosa population. This population are men and women starting from the ages of 15 upwards who are not well conversant in English. The audience is also all men, women and children world-wide from ages +/-15 and upwards who have interest in learning IsiXhosa as an additional language.

TRANSLATION (TRL)

Translate the file ensuring that the doctrines are correct and that translations are faithful to source text, being as literal as possible without losing the meaning of the source. Consult and update word lists. If necessary, start a glossary that will be shared. Check spelling and punctuation. Check the translation guide (TRG) while translating for any clarification and warning, and check the reference materials as needed to verify the context of the translations.

Readiness and Correctness

Check for correctness: names, titles, places, dates, page numbers. etc. Compare table of contents and the text to ensure that they are consistent: Chapter titles, Subtitles, Headings, and subheadings. Compare the key words with the Official Terminology List to ensure they are correctly spelled and check to see that phrases which occur repeatedly through the text have been translated the same way in the same context each time.

Completeness, Consistency and Faithfulness

Compare the target text to the source to ensure that they have the same content. Compare table of contents and the text to ensure that they are consistent: Chapter titles, Subtitles, Headings, and subheadings. Compare the key words with the Official Terminology List to ensure they are correctly spelled and check to see that phrases which occur repeatedly through the text have been translated the same way in the same context.

CONTENT REVIEW (CTR)

Compare the translation to the English (original) and checks the translation for meaning, consistency, completeness, literalness, and accuracy. CTR review compares the segment in the 2 languages (source and target) and makes sure that they make sense.

Readiness and Correctness

Check the translation and make sure that all segments of the source document have been translated and accounted for. The source document may contain some or all of the following information: copyright information, English publication notice, table of contents, introduction, chapter headings, section headings, paragraph headings, paragraphs, sentences, scripture references, picture credits, picture captions.

Completeness, Consistency and Faithfulness

Check the translation in the table of contents with their translation inside the text to ensure that they are the same. Indicate all inconsistencies by adding comments. Check word list words or phrases which occur repeatedly throughout the text to ensure that they have been translated the same way in the same context each time.

LANGUAGE PROOF REVIEW (LPR)

Language reviewer checks the translation for correct grammar, style, and insures it sounds natural. Primary concern should be with the correctness and clarity of the translation. Reads the translation without comparing it to the source text, correcting anything that is hard to understand or is unnatural. Proofreader ensures that the translation is free from errors in spelling, verifies punctuation, scriptures, and other references. Checks for completeness, consistency, and mechanical correctness.

Readiness and Correctness

Use a checklist to ensure the translation is free from errors in spelling, punctuation, and reference. The translation has been read word by word, checking for correct capitalization and punctuation. All text and names that remain in English are spelled correctly each time they appear. All quotes have a beginning and ending curly quote, including single curly quotes. All quotes are made correctly. Ellipses are used correctly.
